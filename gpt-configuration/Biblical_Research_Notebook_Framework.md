# Biblical Research Notebook Framework
Reusable research workflow for developing biblical-theological manuscripts.

## Purpose
Provide a structured environment for exploring theological questions before
they are shaped into a formal academic paper.

## Activation Phrase
Use Biblical Research Notebook Framework.

## Research Phases

### Phase 1 – Problem Identification
Define the theological or interpretive tension.

Questions:
- What doctrinal compression or distortion might exist?
- Which biblical texts appear in tension?

### Phase 2 – Textual Dataset Collection
Collect relevant passages.

Include:
- Old Testament foundations
- New Testament interpretation
- parallel canonical patterns

Output:
dataset tables or verse clusters.

### Phase 3 – Lexical Investigation
Key terms:

- Hebrew lexemes
- Greek lexemes
- semantic range
- frequency across canon

### Phase 4 – Canonical Topology Mapping
Map redemptive movement:

creation → fall → promise → redemption → restoration → consummation

Identify how each text participates in that movement.

### Phase 5 – Interpretive Distortion Analysis
Identify interpretive moves that neutralize biblical teleology.

Examples:

- indicative/imperative dissociation
- causal inversion
- positional-only salvation
- ecclesial functionalism

### Phase 6 – Theological Synthesis
Develop a coherent canonical model.

Tools:

- structural diagrams
- logical chains
- contrast blocks

### Phase 7 – Paraenetic Trajectory
Move from theology to lived implications.

Focus:

- discipleship
- ecclesial practice
- transformation of life
- public witness

### Phase 8 – Manuscript Assembly
Convert research notebook into modular manuscript sections.

Modules:

00_frontmatter
01_introduction
02_dataset
03_interpretive_analysis
04_theological_topology
05_paraenesis
06_ecclesial_implications
07_conclusion

## Output Goal
Produce a modular Quarto manuscript suitable for PDF publication.
